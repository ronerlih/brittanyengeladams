Brittany Engel-Adams

works at the intersection of dance and technology


'

Brittany Engel-Adams works at the intersection of dance and technology. Her work is usually live. It uses online data and software to peel off layers of culture as a way to discover our humanity. Setting live or recorded performances and installations to transform the way we view the world. Brittany holds a Master's degree from The Institute Chorégraphique International, CCN Montpellier Occitanie, in Pyrénées Méditerranée France. Her work was presented at Galerie Thaddaeus Ropac in Paris Pantin France; Symphony Space in New York, New York; Works & Process at The Guggenheim Museum in New York, New York; Movement Research Performance Journal (Issue 55); The New Media Caucus’s ‘Future Bodies’ at Virginia Tech in Blacksburg Virginia. Throughout her career Brittany performed for Ailey II, Big Dance Theater, Donna Uchizono, Moriah Evans, Netta Yerushalmy, Pam Tanowitz, Yvonne Rainer, among others. She performed across disciplines for projects like Punchdrunk’s ‘Sleep No More’ and ‘The White Album’ directed by Lars Jan. She is a co-star in the HBO television series ‘Boardwalk Empire’ and is featured in the film ‘Chiraq’ directed by Spike Lee. Brittany served on the faculty at New York University’s Tisch School of the Arts, Rutgers University’s Mason Gross School of the Arts, Brooklyn Ballet School, Broadway Dance Center, Bates College Summer Intensive, Joffrey Ballet School, and Steps on Broadway. Brittany is a 2023 Artist in Residence at Harvestworks (Technology, Engineering, Art and Music Lab).
