Brittany Engel-Adams

Works at the intersection of Dance and Technology


'

Brittany Engel-Adams is a new media artist working at the intersection of dance and technology. She received her Master's degree from The (ICI) Institute Chorégraphique International, CCN Montpellier, Occitanie / Pyrénées Méditerranée (FR). Her work is usually live, and uses online data and software, to peel off layers of our culture, as a way to discover our humanity. Setting live or recorded performances and installations that mimic, play, and transform our way of viewing the world using code. Brittany’s work was presented at Galerie Thaddaeus Ropac (FR), Symphony Space (NY), Works & Process – The Guggenheim Museum (NY), Movement Research Performance Journal (Issue 55), ‘Future Bodies’ New Media Caucus Conference at Virginia Tech (VA), among other venues. As a dancer Brittany worked for Yvonne Rainer, Pam Tanowitz, Annie-B Parson, Moriah Evans, Donna Uchizono, Netta Yerushalmy, Stefanie Batten Bland, Ailey II, among others. Brittany performed across disciplines for projects like Sleep No More (immersive theater); The White Album (multimedia theater) directed by Lars Jan; 10019 (site-specific performance) choreographed by Naomi Goldberg Haas. She is a co-star in the HBO television series ‘Boardwalk Empire’ and is featured in the film ‘Chiraq’ directed by Spike Lee. Brittany served on the faculty at New York University and Rutgers University. Brittany is a 2023 Artist in Residence at Harvestworks (Technology, Engineering, Art and Music Lab).
