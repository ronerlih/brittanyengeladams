Brittany Engel-Adams

Works at the intersection of Dance and Technology


'

Brittany Engel-Adams is an artist whose work intersects at the junction of dance and technology. Her work is usually live, and uses online data and software to consider some of the urgent questions that might challenge our humanity in the 21st century. Ranging from climate change to the rise of new disruptive technologies (such as artificial intelligence.) In the coming decades the twin revolutions of bio-tech and info-tech merge into a singular scientific tsunami. This might undermine our conception of humanity and shake the humanist foundation of modernist civilization. Brittany’s work was presented by Galerie Thaddaeus Ropac / Paris Pantin (FR), Symphony Space / New York (NY), Rutgers University / New Brunswick (NJ), The Guggenheim Museum’s Works & Process / New York (NY), Movement Research Performance Journal / Issue 55, New Media Caucus at Virginia Tech / Blacksburg (VA), among other venues. Brittany has a Master's degree in Fine Arts, from The University of the Arts' School of Dance Program, at The (ICI) Institute Chorégraphique International, CCN Montpellier – Occitanie / Pyrénées Méditerranée (FR). Throughout her career, Brittany danced for Ailey II, Annie-B Parson, Donna Uchizono, Moriah Evans, Netta Yerushalmy, Pam Tanowitz, Stefanie Batten Bland, among others. Brittany performed across disciplines for projects like Sleep No More (immersive theater); The White Album (multimedia theater) directed by Lars Jan; 10019 (site-specific performance) choreographed by Naomi Goldberg Haas. She is a co-star in the HBO television series ‘Boardwalk Empire’ and is featured in the film ‘Chiraq’ directed by Spike Lee. Brittany served on the faculty at New York University and Rutgers University. Brittany is a 2023 Artist in Residence at Harvestworks (Technology, Engineering, Art and Music Lab).
