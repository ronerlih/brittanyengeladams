Brittany Engel-Adams

New Media Artist


'

Brittany Engel-Adams is a new media artist whose work intersects at the junction of dance and technology. Typically, her work is live, and uses online data and software to consider sum urgent questions that challenge our humanity in the 21st century. Ranging from climate change to the rise of new disruptive technologies (e.g. artificial intelligence) in the coming decades – the twin revolutions of biotech and infotech merge into a singular overwhelming scientific tsunami – This might undermine our conception of humanity, and shake the humanist foundation of modernist civilization. Engel-Adams has presented her work at Galerie Thaddaeus Ropac, in Paris (FR); Symphony Space, in New York (NY); Works & Process at The Guggenheim Museum, in New York (NY); New Media Caucus - ‘Future Bodies’ at Virginia Tech, in Blacksburg (VA); and in The Movement Research Performance Journal, from 2021; among others. Engel-Adams has her Master's degree in Dance, from The University of the Arts' School of Dance Program, at The (ICI) Institute Chorégraphique International, CCN Montpellier, Occitanie Pyrénées Méditerranée (FR). Throughout her career, Engel-Adams performed for Yvonne Rainer, Annie-B Parson of ‘Big Dance Theater’, Donna Uchizono Co., Lars Jan’s ‘Early Morning Opera’, Pam Tanowitz Dance Co., and others. Engel-Adams performed across disciplines for projects, such as ‘Sleep No More’, she is a co-star in the HBO television series ‘Boardwalk Empire’. Engel-Adams served on the faculty at New York University (NY), Bates College (ME), Rutgers University (NJ), Brooklyn Ballet (NY), Broadway Dance Center (NY), Dreyfoos School of the Arts (FL), Studio Naim (Tel Aviv), Joffrey Ballet (NY), Steps on Broadway (NY). Engel-Adams is a 2023 Harvestworks New Works Residency Awardee.
